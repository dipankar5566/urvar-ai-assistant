# Urvar Crop Disease & Deficiency Identification Guide

*For use by the Crop Doctor AI — visual diagnosis reference for West Bengal and eastern India.*
*Dosage unit: 1 katha = 67 sq m. All foliar sprays: apply in the morning or evening, avoid direct sunlight.*

---

## Nutrient Deficiency Visual Guide

**Key rule before diagnosing:**
- Symptoms on **old/lower leaves first** → mobile nutrient (Nitrogen, Phosphorus, Potassium, Magnesium)
- Symptoms on **young/upper leaves first** → immobile nutrient (Iron, Zinc, Boron, Calcium)

---

### Nitrogen (N) Deficiency
**What you see in a photo:** Uniform pale yellow-green colour starting from older bottom leaves, moving upward. Entire leaf yellows (not patchy). Stunted growth, thin spindly stems, small leaves.
**Crops most affected:** Rice, maize, leafy vegetables, potato
**Cause:** Nitrogen leaches quickly in sandy soils and heavy rain; low soil organic matter.
**Urvar treatment:**
- Enriched Vermicompost — 10–15 kg per katha — mix into soil or side-dress
- FYM/Cow Dung Manure — 70–100 kg per katha — incorporate before planting
- PROM Humic Enriched — 2–3 kg per katha — soil application, boosts microbial N-fixation

---

### Phosphorus (P) Deficiency
**What you see in a photo:** Older leaves turn dark green then purplish-red or bronze on the underside (especially on maize, tomato). Delayed maturity, poor root development, thin spindly stems.
**Crops most affected:** Maize, tomato, potato, beans
**Cause:** Phosphorus unavailable in acidic or waterlogged soils; low soil organic matter.
**Urvar treatment:**
- PROM (Phosphate Rich Organic Manure) — 3–5 kg per katha — basal application, mix into soil
- PROM Humic Enriched — 2–3 kg per katha — enhances P solubilisation
- Humic Acid Liquid Bio-Stimulant — 2–3 ml per litre water, soil drench — chelates P for root uptake

---

### Potassium (K) Deficiency
**What you see in a photo:** Older leaf margins and tips turn brown and scorched (tip burn), starting from lower leaves. Leaf edges look burned while centres stay green. Weak stems, lodging in rice/wheat.
**Crops most affected:** Potato, tomato, banana, rice, wheat
**Cause:** Sandy soils or high rainfall leaching K; excessive N application depletes K balance.
**Urvar treatment:**
- Enriched Vermicompost — 10–15 kg per katha — rich in K, improves soil structure
- PROM Humic Enriched — 2–3 kg per katha — improves K uptake efficiency
- Humic Acid Liquid Bio-Stimulant — 2–3 ml per litre, foliar spray — improves nutrient mobilisation

---

### Magnesium (Mg) Deficiency
**What you see in a photo:** Older leaves show bright yellow colour between the veins (interveinal chlorosis) while veins stay green — distinctive fishbone or herringbone pattern. Leaves may curl upward.
**Crops most affected:** Tomato, potato, cucumber, beans
**Cause:** Acidic soils, excess K or Ca application blocks Mg uptake.
**Urvar treatment:**
- Enriched Vermicompost — 10–15 kg per katha — contains available Mg and improves soil pH
- Humic Acid Liquid Bio-Stimulant — 2–3 ml per litre water, foliar spray — improves Mg mobility

---

### Iron (Fe) Deficiency
**What you see in a photo:** Young/new leaves (upper canopy) turn bright yellow while veins remain distinctly green (interveinal chlorosis on young leaves). Severe cases: leaves turn almost white. Lower leaves remain normal green.
**Crops most affected:** Rice, groundnut, soybean, citrus
**Cause:** Alkaline soils (pH > 7.5), waterlogged soils, or excess phosphorus blocks Fe.
**Urvar treatment:**
- Humic Acid Liquid Bio-Stimulant — 2–3 ml per litre water, foliar spray — humic acids chelate Fe and improve availability
- PROM Humic Enriched — 2–3 kg per katha — improves soil pH and Fe chelation

---

### Zinc (Zn) Deficiency
**What you see in a photo:** Young leaves show interveinal chlorosis with a bronze or brown tint. Rice: "khaira disease" — white/pale patches on young leaves, stunted growth, no tillering. Maize: pale striping on young leaves. Tomato/potato: small distorted leaves, shortened internodes.
**Crops most affected:** Rice (khaira), maize, potato, wheat, tomato
**Cause:** Alkaline or calcareous soils, flooded conditions in rice, high phosphorus blocks Zn.
**Urvar treatment:**
- Zinc EDTA 12% — **3–5 gm per 20 litres water** — foliar spray at 20–25 DAT and again at 40–45 DAT
- For rice khaira: spray at transplanting + 25 DAT + 45 DAT for best results
- Soil application: 2–3 gm Zinc EDTA per litre of water, drench around root zone

---

### Boron (B) Deficiency
**What you see in a photo:** Growing tips die back (tip dieback), young leaves thicken and curl, hollow stems (broccoli, cauliflower), poor fruit set, distorted or cracked fruits, empty pods in mustard. Onion: distorted bulb with brown discoloration inside.
**Crops most affected:** Cauliflower, broccoli, mustard, sunflower, onion, cotton
**Cause:** Sandy soils, dry conditions, high rainfall leaching, liming raises pH.
**Urvar treatment:**
- Boron EDTA — **2–3 gm per 20 litres water** — foliar spray at flower bud stage and again at early fruiting
- For onion: spray at 30 DAT and 50 DAT
- For mustard: spray at flower bud initiation and 50% flowering stage

## Common Crop Diseases — West Bengal & Eastern India

### Rice Diseases

**Blast (Pyricularia oryzae)**
*What you see:* Diamond-shaped lesions with grey-white centres and brown-red borders on leaves. Lesions have a pointed "spindle" shape. In severe cases, lesions merge, leaves dry up. Neck blast: panicle neck turns brown-black, grain doesn't fill.
*Cause:* Fungal disease; favoured by cloudy weather, high humidity, heavy nitrogen application.
*Urvar support:* Vermicompost + PROM Humic Enriched as basal (builds plant immunity); note: a fungicide is also needed — inform farmer to consult a KVK or agri-input dealer for fungicide recommendation. Urvar products support plant health and recovery.

**Brown Spot (Helminthosporium oryzae)**
*What you see:* Circular to oval brown spots with yellow halo on leaves. Spots have a brown border with a lighter grey-brown centre. Infected grains are brown and shrivelled (stackburn).
*Cause:* Nutrient deficiency (especially K and Mn) makes rice susceptible; fungal pathogen.
*Urvar treatment:* Enriched Vermicompost 10–15 kg/katha + PROM 3–5 kg/katha as basal to correct nutrient deficiency. Zinc EDTA foliar spray reduces susceptibility.

**Sheath Blight (Rhizoctonia solani)**
*What you see:* Irregular greenish-grey water-soaked lesions on leaf sheath near waterline. Lesions enlarge and turn greyish-white with brown borders. Sclerotia (small dark brown mustard-like bodies) visible on affected tissue.
*Cause:* Soil-borne fungus; dense planting, high N fertilizer, and standing water promote spread.
*Urvar support:* Vermicompost and PROM improve soil microbial balance which suppresses soilborne pathogens. Reduce N application if overloaded.

---

### Potato Diseases

**Late Blight (Phytophthora infestans)**
*What you see:* Water-soaked dark brown/black lesions on leaves, often starting at leaf tips or margins. White cottony fungal growth visible on underside of lesions in humid conditions. Lesions spread rapidly in cool, wet weather. Tubers show brown rot inside.
*Cause:* Oomycete pathogen; spreads rapidly in cool (15–20°C) humid conditions. Very destructive.
*Urvar support:* Vermicompost basal application improves plant immunity; humic acid foliar spray strengthens cell walls. A systemic fungicide is essential — inform farmer urgently (CRITICAL severity).

**Early Blight (Alternaria solani)**
*What you see:* Circular to oval dark brown lesions with concentric rings (target-board pattern) on older leaves. Yellow halo around lesions. Starts from lower leaves, moves upward.
*Cause:* Fungal disease; stress (water, nutrient) makes plants vulnerable.
*Urvar treatment:* Zinc EDTA 3–5 gm in 20L water, foliar spray — zinc reduces fungal susceptibility. Vermicompost + PROM basal strengthens plant.

**Black Scurf (Rhizoctonia solani on potato)**
*What you see:* Black crusty patches on tuber skin (not inside). Stems have brown cankers at base. Stolons may be killed, reducing tuber numbers.
*Cause:* Soil-borne fungus; worse in cold wet soils.
*Urvar treatment:* Vermicompost 10–15 kg per katha + PROM basal — improves soil biology and suppresses Rhizoctonia. Humic Acid soil drench before planting.

---

### Tomato / Brinjal / Chilli Diseases

**Early Blight (Alternaria solani on tomato)**
*What you see:* Dark brown target-board (concentric rings) lesions on older leaves, stem, and fruit. Yellow halo surrounds lesions. Starts from bottom leaves, works upward.
*Urvar treatment:* Zinc EDTA 3–5 gm in 20L, foliar spray 2–3 times at 7-day intervals. Vermicompost 15–20 kg per katha as basal.

**Fusarium Wilt (Fusarium oxysporum)**
*What you see:* Plant wilts on one side or entirely, even when soil is moist. Yellowing of lower leaves first, then whole plant. Cut the stem — brown discolouration visible inside the vascular tissue.
*Cause:* Soil-borne fungal pathogen; spreads through infected soil and water.
*Urvar treatment:* Enriched Vermicompost 15–20 kg per katha (improves Trichoderma populations that suppress Fusarium); Humic Acid 2–3 ml per litre soil drench at planting.

**Leaf Curl Virus (TYLCV — Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus)**
*What you see:* Young leaves curl upward and inward (cup-shaped), turn yellow, smaller than normal. Stunted plant, no fruit set. Transmitted by whitefly.
*Cause:* Viral disease spread by whitefly. No cure — infected plants must be removed.
*Urvar support:* Control whitefly vector with sticky traps. Vermicompost + PROM Humic Enriched builds plant vigour which reduces virus severity in remaining plants.

**Damping Off (Pythium/Rhizoctonia)**
*What you see:* Seedlings collapse at soil level — stem appears water-soaked and pinched at the base. Seedlings fall over suddenly. Common in seedling trays and nurseries.
*Cause:* Soil-borne fungi; overwatering and poor drainage trigger it.
*Urvar treatment:* Mix Enriched Vermicompost into nursery soil at 20% by volume. Humic Acid 2 ml per litre water drench in nursery beds.

---

### Mustard Diseases

**White Rust (Albugo candida)**
*What you see:* White powdery/chalky pustules (blisters) on underside of leaves; corresponding yellow patches on upper side. Infected stems and pods become distorted and swollen ("staghead").
*Cause:* Oomycete pathogen; cool humid weather.
*Urvar support:* Boron EDTA 2–3 gm/20L water foliar spray at flower bud stage strengthens tissue and reduces white rust incidence. Vermicompost basal improves plant immunity.

**Alternaria Blight (Alternaria brassicae)**
*What you see:* Dark brown/black circular spots with concentric rings on leaves, stem, and pods. Yellow halo. Spots on pods cause premature shattering and yield loss.
*Cause:* Fungal disease; spreads in humid conditions during flowering.
*Urvar treatment:* Zinc EDTA 3–5 gm in 20L, foliar spray. Humic Acid 2–3 ml per litre foliar to strengthen cell walls.

---

### Onion Diseases

**Purple Blotch (Alternaria porri)**
*What you see:* Small water-soaked lesions that enlarge and turn brown then purple with a yellow halo. Lesions girdle the leaf causing tip dieback. White powdery spores visible in humid weather.
*Cause:* Fungal; warm humid conditions during leaf development.
*Urvar treatment:* Zinc EDTA 3–5 gm in 20L water foliar spray. Boron EDTA 2–3 gm in 20L foliar spray at 30 DAT and 50 DAT.

**Stemphylium Blight (Stemphylium vesicarium)**
*What you see:* Small yellow to tan water-soaked lesions on leaves that enlarge into irregular brown lesions. Tips die back. Differs from purple blotch by absence of purple tint and more irregular shape.
*Urvar treatment:* Zinc EDTA + Boron EDTA foliar spray as above. Vermicompost 10–15 kg/katha basal for general immunity.

---

### Wheat Diseases

**Yellow Rust / Stripe Rust (Puccinia striiformis)**
*What you see:* Bright yellow-orange powdery pustules arranged in neat stripes along leaf veins. Pustules are small and linear. Leaves turn pale yellow. Cool temperatures (10–15°C) and dew favour spread.

**Brown Rust / Leaf Rust (Puccinia triticina)**
*What you see:* Orange-brown round to oval powdery pustules randomly scattered on upper leaf surface (not in stripes). No necrotic border. Common during warmer, humid periods.

**Black Rust / Stem Rust (Puccinia graminis)**
*What you see:* Large brick-red to dark brown oval pustules on both leaf surface and stems. Pustules break open to reveal rusty brown powder. Most destructive.

*For all rust diseases:*
*Urvar support:* Vermicompost + PROM basal (strong plants resist rusts better); Zinc EDTA foliar spray. A fungicide is required for rust control — inform farmer.

---

### Cucurbit Diseases (Cucumber, Bitter Gourd, Bottle Gourd, Watermelon)

**Powdery Mildew (Erysiphe cichoracearum)**
*What you see:* White powdery coating on upper leaf surface, starting as small circular patches spreading to cover entire leaf. Leaves yellow and dry up. Does NOT require wet conditions — thrives in dry weather.
*Urvar treatment:* Humic Acid 3 ml per litre water foliar spray — acidifies leaf surface, inhibits powdery mildew. Zinc EDTA 3–5 gm in 20L. Vermicompost basal for plant vigour.

**Downy Mildew (Pseudoperonospora cubensis)**
*What you see:* Angular yellow patches on upper leaf surface (bounded by leaf veins, angular not round). Grey-purple mould on underside of patches in humid conditions. Spreads rapidly in wet, cool weather.
*Urvar support:* Avoid overhead irrigation. Vermicompost + PROM basal. Humic Acid foliar to strengthen stomata. A fungicide (mancozeb or metalaxyl) is needed.

## Pest Damage Visual Patterns

### Aphids
*What you see:* Clusters of tiny soft-bodied insects (green, black, or brown) on undersides of young leaves and shoot tips. Leaves curl, pucker, and turn yellow. Sticky honeydew on leaves with sooty mould growth.
*Crops:* Mustard, tomato, chilli, beans, wheat
*Urvar support:* Vermicompost + PROM Humic Enriched for strong plant growth. Note: a neem-based or chemical insecticide is needed to control aphids directly.

### Whitefly
*What you see:* Tiny white moth-like insects fly up in clouds when plant is disturbed. Leaves pale yellow with sticky honeydew coating. Sooty mould on leaves. Transmits leaf curl virus.
*Crops:* Tomato, brinjal, chilli, cotton, beans
*Urvar support:* Healthy plants (Vermicompost + Humic Acid) tolerate whitefly better. Insecticide or yellow sticky traps needed for control.

### Thrips
*What you see:* Leaves show silvery-white streaks or patches; leaf surface appears scratched or stippled. In onions: silvery blotches on leaves. Young leaves may be distorted. Tiny slender insects (0.5–1mm) visible with magnifying glass.
*Crops:* Onion, chilli, beans, groundnut
*Urvar support:* Boron EDTA foliar spray at early stage strengthens cell walls making them less susceptible. Insecticide needed for control.

### Leaf Miner (Liriomyza spp.)
*What you see:* Characteristic white winding tunnels/trails on leaves — the mine appears as a meandering white line on the leaf surface. Larvae visible inside the mine if leaf held to light.
*Crops:* Tomato, potato, beans, pea
*Urvar support:* Vermicompost + PROM for strong plant recovery. Insecticide needed.

### Stem Borer (various moths)
*What you see in rice:* "Dead heart" — central shoot of young tiller turns yellow/brown and pulls out easily. "White ear" — panicle turns white while rest of crop is green. Entry holes at base of tillers.
*What you see in maize:* Irregular windowing on leaves, frass (sawdust-like excrement) in whorl, broken stalks.
*Urvar support:* Vermicompost + PROM basal improves plant strength. Insecticide or pheromone trap needed.

## Soil Health Visual Indicators

### Waterlogging / Poor Drainage
*What you see:* Yellowing of entire plant (looks like N deficiency but doesn't respond to N), wilting despite moist soil, roots turn brown and slimy, foul smell from soil. Bluish-grey soil colour below topsoil indicates anaerobic conditions.
*Urvar treatment:* Improve drainage. Apply Vermicompost 15–20 kg/katha to improve soil structure and aeration. Humic Acid 3 ml per litre soil drench helps restore aerobic microbial activity.

### Soil Crust / Compaction
*What you see:* Hard sealed surface after irrigation or rain. Poor seedling emergence, roots remain shallow. Cracked polygonal patterns on surface when dry.
*Urvar treatment:* Vermicompost 15–20 kg/katha incorporated before planting breaks compaction layers. Humic Acid soil drench 2–3 ml per litre improves soil aggregation.

### Low Organic Matter (Degraded Soil)
*What you see:* Pale grey/tan soil colour (vs. dark brown of healthy soil). Poor water retention — soil dries out quickly and cracks. Plants generally unthrifty, multiple deficiency symptoms simultaneously.
*Urvar treatment:* FYM/Cow Dung Manure 70–100 kg/katha + Vermicompost 10–15 kg/katha + PROM 3–5 kg/katha as basal. Apply every season to build organic matter.

### Salinity Stress
*What you see:* Leaf tip and margin burn (similar to K deficiency but on all leaves). White crusty salt deposits on soil surface. Plants stunted with dark green waxy appearance. Roots brown and stunted.
*Urvar treatment:* Humic Acid 3 ml per litre soil drench — humic acids improve salt tolerance and chelate excess Na. Vermicompost 15–20 kg/katha improves soil water balance.
