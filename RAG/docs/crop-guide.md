## Detailed Crop Programs

### Paddy (Rice) — Kharif Season

| Growth Stage | Product | Dose per Katha | Method |
|-------------|---------|---------------|--------|
| Land preparation (7–10 days before puddling) | Vermicompost | 17–25 kg | Broadcast and incorporate into soil |
| Land preparation | FYM | 35–50 kg | Broadcast and incorporate |
| Final puddling / transplanting | PROM | 1.5–2 kg | Incorporate into puddled soil |
| Tillering stage (25–30 DAT) | Humic Acid Liquid | 15–25 ml in 20L water | Soil drench / drip |
| Pre-flowering / booting (if boron deficiency suspected) | Boron EDTA | 2–4 gm in 20L water | Foliar spray |

**Why Boron for Paddy?** Boron deficiency in rice causes grain sterility and reduces productive tillers. In WB lateritic soils, boron response in paddy has been documented at 52.5% yield improvement. Pre-flowering application is essential on sandy or lateritic soils.

### Potato — Rabi Season

| Growth Stage | Product | Dose per Katha | Method |
|-------------|---------|---------------|--------|
| Bed preparation (before planting) | Vermicompost | 25–30 kg | Mix into top 6 inches of soil |
| Bed preparation | FYM | 50–60 kg | Mix into top 6 inches of soil |
| Bed preparation | PROM Humic Enriched | 2–2.5 kg | Mix into soil |
| Vegetative growth (30 DAP) | Humic Acid Liquid | 15–25 ml in 20L water | Soil drench |
| Tuber bulking (if zinc deficiency) | Zinc EDTA | 3–7 gm in 20L water | Foliar spray |

**Note:** Potato is a heavy feeder. The combination of Vermicompost + FYM + PROM ensures slow-release nutrition across the 90–120 day season without the soil acidification caused by repeated chemical NPK application.

### Tomato / Brinjal / Chilli — Vegetables

| Growth Stage | Product | Dose per Katha | Method |
|-------------|---------|---------------|--------|
| Before transplanting (basal) | Vermicompost | 25–35 kg | Mix into beds |
| Before transplanting | PROM Humic Enriched | 2–2.5 kg | Mix into beds |
| Vegetative growth (2–3 weeks after transplanting) | Humic Acid Liquid | 30–60 ml in 20L water | Foliar spray or soil drench |
| 10–15 days before flowering | PROM Flowering Booster | 2–2.5 kg | Top dress around root zone |
| At first flower bud stage | Boron EDTA | 2–4 gm in 20L water | Foliar spray |
| Repeat Boron if fruit set is poor | Boron EDTA | 2–4 gm in 20L water | Foliar spray (15 days after first spray) |

**Why Boron at Flowering?** Boron is essential for pollen tube elongation and fertilization. Without adequate boron at the flowering stage, flower drop increases and fruit set drops. This is especially important for Chilli and Tomato in WB soils.

---

### Cauliflower / Cabbage (Brassicas) — Rabi

| Growth Stage | Product | Dose per Katha | Method |
|-------------|---------|---------------|--------|
| Bed preparation | FYM | 50–65 kg | Mix into soil |
| Bed preparation | PROM | 2–2.5 kg | Mix into soil |
| 3 weeks after transplanting | Humic Acid Liquid | 30–60 ml in 20L water | Foliar spray |
| Head formation stage (critical) | Boron EDTA | 2–4 gm in 20L water | Foliar spray |
| Repeat 15 days later | Boron EDTA | 2–4 gm in 20L water | Foliar spray |

**Why Boron is Critical for Brassicas:** Cauliflower and cabbage are among the most boron-sensitive crops. Boron deficiency causes hollow stem in cauliflower (browning, water-soaked internal cavities), brown curd, and poor head formation. In WB red and lateritic soils, boron deficiency in brassicas is very common. Two foliar applications of Boron EDTA are recommended as a preventive measure.

### Mango / Guava (Fruit Trees) — Annual Program

| Timing | Product | Dose per Tree | Method |
|--------|---------|--------------|--------|
| Post-harvest (after fruit collection) | Vermicompost | 5–8 kg per tree | Apply in ring around drip line |
| Post-harvest | FYM | 8–12 kg per tree | Apply in ring, mix into top 3 inches |
| Post-harvest | PROM | 400–500 gm per tree | Mix into ring application |
| Pre-flowering (1 month before) | PROM Flowering Booster | 500 gm per tree | Apply around root zone |
| At flower bud stage | Boron EDTA | 2–4 gm in 20L water | Foliar spray |
| At flowering / fruit set | Humic Acid Liquid | 30–60 ml in 20L water | Foliar spray |

**Note for Mango:** Boron deficiency causes poor panicle emergence and low fruit set. Pre-flowering Boron EDTA application is highly recommended in WB soils.

### Banana — Year-Round Program

| Growth Stage | Product | Dose per Plant | Method |
|-------------|---------|---------------|--------|
| Planting / sucker establishment | Vermicompost | 2–3 kg per plant | Mix into planting pit |
| Planting / sucker establishment | FYM | 4–5 kg per plant | Mix into planting pit |

### Marigold / Tuberose (Flowers)

| Growth Stage | Product | Dose per Katha | Method |
|-------------|---------|---------------|--------|
| Bed preparation | Vermicompost | 25 kg | Mix into soil |
| Bed preparation | PROM Humic Enriched | 2 kg | Mix into soil |
| 15–20 days after transplanting | Humic Acid Liquid | 30–60 ml in 20L water | Foliar spray |
| 10–15 days before flowering | PROM Flowering Booster | 2 kg | Top dress around root zone |
| At first flower bud | Boron EDTA | 2–4 gm in 20L water | Foliar spray |

**Note:** Marigold is extremely responsive to phosphorus during bud initiation. The PROM Flowering Booster applied 10–15 days before expected flowering gives the best results for uniform, larger blooms.

### Mustard / Oilseeds — Rabi

| Growth Stage | Product | Dose per Katha | Method |
|-------------|---------|---------------|--------|
| Land preparation | FYM | 40–50 kg | Mix into soil |
| At sowing | PROM | 1.5–1.7 kg | Mix into seed bed |
| Rosette stage | Humic Acid Liquid | 15–25 ml in 20L water | Soil drench |
| Pre-flowering (bud stage — critical) | Boron EDTA | 2–4 gm in 20L water | Foliar spray |

**Why Boron is Critical for Mustard:** Boron deficiency in mustard causes failure of pollen viability and hollow stems, directly reducing pod set and seed yield. It is one of the most boron-sensitive crops grown in WB. Pre-flowering application of Boron EDTA is strongly recommended.

### Nursery / Seedling Beds

| Stage | Product | Dose | Method |
|-------|---------|------|--------|
| Bed preparation | Vermicompost | 35 kg per katha | Mix thoroughly with topsoil (1:3 ratio) |
| After seed germination (7–10 days) | Humic Acid Liquid | 15–20 ml in 20L water | Light soil drench (avoid waterlogging) |
| Before transplanting (7 days prior) | Humic Acid Liquid | 15–20 ml in 20L water | Soil drench to strengthen root mass |

**Note:** Do not apply PROM, FYM or Zinc/Boron EDTA directly to seedling beds — concentrate on Vermicompost and Humic Acid at this stage. PROM and micronutrients are best applied at main field transplanting.

### Home Garden — Pots & Terrace Beds

#### Potting Mix Base Recipe (per 10-inch pot)
- Cocopeat or garden soil: 70%
- Vermicompost: 25% (approx. 150–200g)
- PROM Humic Enriched: 10g (a level tablespoon)
- Mix thoroughly before filling the pot

#### Ongoing Maintenance (every 30–45 days)
| Application | Product | Dose per Pot (10-inch) |
|-------------|---------|----------------------|
| Top dressing (monthly) | Vermicompost | 1–2 handfuls (50–100g) |
| Foliar spray (fortnightly) | Humic Acid Liquid | 2–3 ml in 1 litre water |
| Pre-flowering (once per season) | PROM Flowering Booster | 1 teaspoon (5g) worked into topsoil |
| If yellowing between veins | Zinc EDTA | 0.5 gm in 1 litre water, foliar spray |
| If flower drop or poor fruit set | Boron EDTA | 0.3 gm in 1 litre water, foliar spray |

#### Beginner Starter Recommendation
For someone starting their home garden with no prior experience with organic fertilizers:
1. **Start with Vermicompost** — mix into potting medium before planting. It is the safest, most versatile product and provides a broad-spectrum nutrient and microbial foundation.
2. **Add Humic Acid Liquid** — spray every 2 weeks on leaves and soil. Visible improvement in leaf color and plant vigor within 1–2 weeks.
3. **Use PROM Flowering Booster** when plants begin to show flower buds — apply once per season for improved flower and fruit set.
4. **Use Zinc EDTA or Boron EDTA** only if you see specific deficiency symptoms (interveinal yellowing = zinc; flower drop = boron).

## Deficiency Symptom Quick Reference

| Symptom | Likely Deficiency | Recommended Product |
|---------|------------------|-------------------|
| Yellowing between leaf veins (green veins, yellow between) | Zinc | Zinc EDTA 12% |
| Small, narrow, stunted new leaves | Zinc | Zinc EDTA 12% |
| Flower drop / poor fruit set | Boron | Boron EDTA |
| Hollow stem in cauliflower / broccoli | Boron | Boron EDTA |
| Cracked, deformed fruits | Boron | Boron EDTA |
| Overall pale, low-vigor plant with dark green older leaves | Phosphorus | PROM or PROM Humic Enriched |
| General nutrient deficiency, low vigor, poor soil | All macro/micro | Vermicompost + Humic Acid Liquid |
| Soil compaction, poor water retention | Organic matter deficit | Vermicompost + FYM |
